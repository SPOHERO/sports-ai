About the Center

This site is designed as a central place to present Auburn women’s soccer analytics work, including performance analysis, player evaluation, match intelligence, and AI-supported research projects.

Mission

Support competitive performance

Translate raw match and training data into clear insights that can support tactical review, player development, and evidence-based decision making.

Research

Bridge sports and AI

Build a platform where sports analytics, statistics, and machine learning come together through practical, interpretable applications in women’s soccer.

Education

Create hands-on student opportunities

Involve students in real-world data workflows, visualization, modeling, and reporting for applied learning in modern sports data science.

Research Focus

The center can grow around several complementary analytics directions, from basic match reporting to advanced AI-based modeling.

Match & Team Performance

  • Possession structure and territorial control
  • Shot quality and chance creation
  • Pressing and defensive recovery patterns
  • Passing networks and build-up sequences

Player Analytics

  • Role-based player profiles
  • Movement intensity and workload
  • Spatial tendencies and heatmaps
  • Development trends across time

AI & Predictive Modeling

  • Event prediction and sequence modeling
  • Player tracking and trajectory analysis
  • Automated report generation
  • Video-to-insight pipelines

Data Pipeline

A simple, credible structure for the center is to show how raw data becomes actionable insight.

1. Collect Match video, event logs, player tracking, training data, and performance summaries.
2. Clean Standardize timestamps, align player identifiers, remove noise, and validate data quality.
3. Model Estimate metrics, detect patterns, build predictive models, and compare player or team profiles.
4. Visualize Create heatmaps, trend charts, dashboards, match reports, and role-based summaries.
5. Act Deliver interpretable insights for coaches, staff, students, and research collaborators.
